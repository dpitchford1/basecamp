# Content Team Overview

Welcome to the Basecamp CMS. This section explains how the website is structured and how to work with it effectively.

---

## How the Site Is Organized

The website has four main content areas, each managed from the WordPress admin:

| Section | Admin Menu | What It Controls |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Sectors** | Sectors | The design sector pages (theme parks, waterparks, etc.) |
| **Services** | Pages → Services | The services listed on the Services page |
| **News** | News | News posts and articles |
| **Pages** | Pages | Standard pages (Home, Our Story, Contact, etc.) |

---

## Getting Around the Admin

After logging in you'll see the main admin menu on the left. The most important areas for content work are:

- **News** — add, edit, and manage news posts
- **Sectors** — manage the design sector pages and their galleries
- **Pages** — edit static pages (About, Contact, etc.)
- **Media → Media Library** — upload and manage images inside real folders
- **Mi Docs** — this documentation viewer

---

## Saving & Publishing

- **Publish** — makes the content live immediately
- **Save Draft** — saves without publishing
- **Schedule** — set a future publish date
- **Update** — saves changes to already-published content

> Always click **Update** or **Publish** after making changes. Navigating away without saving will lose your work.

---

## Images & Media

- Images are organised into **real folders** in the Media Library — not tags or virtual categories. See [Managing Media & Folders](02-media.md) for a full walkthrough.
- Upload images via **Media → Media Library** (not the default WordPress uploader — the folder system lives there)
- Recommended minimum width: **1400px** for full-width images
- The site automatically converts uploaded images to WebP format for faster loading — you do not need to do anything special
- For gallery images in Sectors, drag to reorder

---

## Getting Help

If something doesn't look right on the live site after publishing, check:

1. Did you click **Update** / **Publish**?
2. Is the correct page template selected? (check Page Attributes in the sidebar)
3. Is the metabox data saved correctly? (scroll down to find custom metaboxes below the editor)

For technical issues, contact the developer.
